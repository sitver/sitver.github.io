By Michael Sitver (on Twitter, Product Hunt)


Product Hunt is my favorite website. I check it every morning, and every afternoon. I upvote. I comment. But since it's carefully curated, I often run out of products quickly.


Luckily, today I learned about a separate, secret section of Product Hunt. A section for the Product Hunt power-users. The "Upcoming" section.


The "Upcoming" section is where Product Hunt posts wait in limbo for a following, or for a moderator to promote it to the main site. Among today's posts is a drone-advertising business, special bike shorts, and a collaborative prototyping app.


In "Upcoming" are brilliant startups that just haven't made it to the homepage yet, as well as terrible startups, from spammy blogs, to genuine products that solve really weird, niche problems.


Unlike on the homepage, where you are a voter and the content has already been filtered, on "Upcoming" you are the decider. You are the curator. You can give posts life.


You are usually the first to see each idea, and you have the opportunity to post the first comment (if you have commenting privileges) or give a post the first upvote.


Sure, I still prefer visiting the active, curated homepage to the "Upcoming" page, but reading through the "Upcoming" page is definitely a fun way to spend a few minutes each day.


(Pro Tip: For a good time, go to the "Upcoming" page with a friend and place bets as to which Products will succeed)
