New York - Today HP unveiled a free 3D scanning app for Sprout, HP's all-in-one touch PC.

In this video, I received a hands-on demo and tour from Mario Pacini, who works on HP's Sprout team.

{{ site.theme.header_text }}

HP's 3D scanning app uses a special 3D camera built into the Sprout to capture the texture, color, and dimensions of an object. 

To create a full scan, users will need to rotate the object by hand, several times, to capture every element of the object. This can be done by hand, but HP will also soon sell a nifty rotating 3D capture stage which will turn 3D scanning into a one-touch, 20-minute process.

Once scanned, objects can be manipulated, shared, or 3D-printed.

The HP Sprout runs $1,899. The 3D capture stage  will run $299, and will be available in July, when the 3D capture software becomes available.
