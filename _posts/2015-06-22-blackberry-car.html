Blackberry's days of smartphone dominance are long over, but surprisingly the company still has a few brilliant, valuable technologies in other verticals.

QNX Software Systems, a division of Blackberry acquired in 2010, builds high-tech car systems for auto manufacturers. 

To demonstrate its latest technology, the QNX team built an insane concept car based on the Maserati  Quattroporte GTS.

The QNX team took the $150,000 car and retrofitted it with brand new discrete systems, video screens, and a beautiful user interface that perhaps puts even Tesla to shame.

{{ site.theme.header_text }}

Among the many modifications, QNX replaced every mirror in the car with an equivalent digital screen. The screens show live feeds from cameras mounted around the car, practically eliminating common blind spots.

QNX's tricked-out car isn't commercially available (yet), but the QNX platform is the basis for many car entertainment and control systems, so many of the technologies seen here should soon be coming to a car near you.
