Paralysis shouldn’t stop children from having an enjoyable halloween… This is the message that parents Ryan and Lana Weimer have been spreading for the past few years to their three, wheel-chair-confined children, all of whom suffer from a rare form of muscular dystrophy called Spinal Muscular Atrophy.

Mr. Weimer has been hard at work for the past few years, building costumes like this “How To Train Your Dragon”-themed wheelchair that allow his kids to have magical halloweens, despite their disability. 

 Now, Ryan wants to spread the joy beyond his own family. That’s why he and his wife Lana launched an organization called “Magic Wheelchair”. According to Ryan, the organization’s goal is to “put a smile on the face of every child in a wheelchair”. 

 For obvious reasons, I’m all for it, which is why I wanted to raise awareness of a new Kickstarter campaign launched by the Weimer organization. 

{{ site.theme.header_text }}

 They set out to raise $15,000, to build five intense costumes for five more wheelchair-confined kids. With 16 days left in their campaign, the Weimers have already raised $17,450, on their way to providing another costumer (each costs about $3,000 to produce).

I’m calling on the internet to give what they can, to make halloween magical for even more children. For each additional $3,000 they raise, the Weimers will be able to build another costume. 

Even  $15 (what I plan to give), multiplied by the power of the internet, can easily build a few more chairs.

Look back on your own halloween memories, then think about how special those memories would be to these children, who don't get to experience so much of life.

Internet: Let’s make halloween special for these kids.
