When it comes to charging your iPhone on the go, there are a million and one different gizmos and doo-dads (official term) for sale to do it.

 

Having tried about a million such devices myself (Ok. Slight exaggeration there), I’ve found that there is no one best charger. It’s entirely dependent on your needs, your budget, and your taste.

That being said, those looking for a solid, affordable dock-style charger with built-in juice, should be very pleased with the $30 Ventev Chargedock 2000 (the company claims this retails for $40, but I was able to find them for $30).

The Chargedock 2000 is, as the name implies, a charging dock — a device that serves as both a phone/iPod stand, and a charger —  with a 2000 miliAmp-hour battery.

The design:

It’s designed in the shape of a little couch cushion, and it’s extremely portable. It’s about twice as thick as an iphone, and about half as long. It’s coated with a soft, rubberish plastic that adds a bit of durability to the dock, and makes it comfortable to tote in your pocket (the lack of sharp corners helps with that as well).

Flip the cover of the Chargedock open, and you’ll find a single lightning connector, angled slightly like a picture frame, allowing iPhones, iPods, and iPads to rest comfortably on a rubber backstop. Docks like these act as kick-stands, allowing you to use your phone as it charges, without having to hold it.

On the back of the Chargedock are a micro-usb port and a single switch. The switch turns on and off the charger. The on/off state is indicated by a row of four little indicator lights. These lights also alert you to how much charge the battery holds.

The Good:

In my time using the Chargedock, I used it mostly while tethered to my laptop, on my desk. It works perfectly well as a regular charger, and it allows easy access to my phone. 

When I brought it on the go though, it really shined. It fit nicely into my pocket, my car, and my backpack, and provided me with the juice I needed to get through the day. The battery indicator was a nice touch, allowing me to keep tabs on when I needed a recharge.

At 1A of charge, this thing can zap a battery from red numbers (almost empty), to green numbers (reasonably full) in very short order.

I also appreciat that the included micro-usb cable was nice and long (about 1 meter). I absolutely hate when battery companies give you a foot-long cord to charge your devices, as if that’s enough length. I know. I care too much about the little things.

The Bad:

Still, a charging dock like this isn’t right for everyone. Since it’s a pretty sizeable stand, you can’t use it to charge your phone while in your pocket. You need to find a place (or at least a bag) to set your phone and the dock down in. You need space. If this is a problem for you, you might be better served by products like Mophie’s Juicepack charging cases, or phonesuit’s Flex charger.

 2,000 miliamp hours also isn’t a lot of extra juice for a device of this size. It will certainly help you out in a pinch, but it won’t last you an entire camping trip, or a long day of travel, or charging for multiple devices. It will provide a bit more than one full charge for most modern iPhones.

I should also note that with my iPhone 5S I had some trouble fitting my phone on the dock with its case. With the case off, it was no problem, and with thinner cases, it wasn’t a problem. I also had no trouble fitting my iPad Mini, with its case, onto the dock.

Conclusion:

For everyday power-users who run their phone batteries down quickly every day, the Ventev Chargedock is an excellent option for a little mid-day fill-up. Even as just a charging dock, the Chargedock is an excellent tool. But if you’re looking for a charger with a lot of juice, or pocketability, you should look elsewhere.
