It's hard to deny the beauty of a unibody aluminum Macbook.

It's sleek, premium, but not really personal. There are millions just like it, and in the coffee shop, you are just another guy (or gal) with a glowing Apple on his laptop.

This week I discovered a company that customized Macbook Pro's in a new, stunningly beautiful, but extremely expensive way.

The company I'm talking about, Uncover, laser-cuts Macbooks to your specifications, creating glowing original logos and artwork on the cover of your laptop. Uncover can even add color to the translucent, glowing artwork of your choice.

How does it work?

Well, Uncover founder Victor Vandoorn took to Product Hunt to describe his creation. "The inspiration came when I noticed that, when you turn down the brightness on a Mac screen, the backlit Apple logo also becomes dimmer.

Previously, I always thought that it was lit by a special LED, but actually, it's the screen's backlight. That meant there was a massive lightsource behind the aluminium, waiting to be 'uncovered'".

Thirty prototypes later, Vandoorn and his team had developed a method for cutting glowing custom artwork into a Macbook's body.

It's a three step process: the laptop owner collaborates with Vandoorn's designers to come up with a design. The Uncover team then creates a transulcent plexiglass-and-aluminum inlay. Lastly, they remove the screen from the Macbook, laser-cut design elements into the Macbook, insert the inlay, and reassemble the computer.

Among his customers, Vandoorn claims to have customized Macbooks for "the CEO's of Evernote, Snapchat, Flipboard, Lyft, [and] The Next Web" as well as various DJs.

As beautiful as Uncover's work is, I doubt you or I will be sending in our laptops anytime soon, unless you sleep in a bathtub of cash. The cutting process will cost you at least 399 euros, approximately $450. Users have to send their laptops into Uncover's studio in Amsterdam, the Netherlands. The process itself takes four days, but you'll be without your laptop for at least a week.

The process also voids your warranty with Apple. Uncover offers a free 6-month warranty on your screen, but other than that, you're on your own for the life of your laptop.

Still, for those who can afford it, Uncover's process, paired with its software (which makes your logo "pulse") is the ultimate way to customize a Macbook.

I'll stick to decals for now (although I might pick up one of Uncover's other custom products).
