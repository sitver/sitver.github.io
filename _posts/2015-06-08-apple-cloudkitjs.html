Apple wants you to store your startup web-app's (yes web app) data with iCloud, and they're giving you a petabyte of free storage to do so. (For those scoring at home, a petabyte is 1 million gigabytes).

According to a calculator available on Apple's website, that petabyte should be enough for about 10 million free users.

Cloudkit.js also comes with a dashboard for watching and managing usage, including total users, active users, and database storage.

The interface is very reminiscent of Parse, a similar developer service bought a few years ago by Facebook to strengthen its own developer eco-system.

It's a really unprecedented move, but one that makes sense for Apple in this competitive landscape. Amazon, Google, Microsoft, and Facebook have been courting startups to their own ecosystems with large discounts, so that they can keep a close eye on hot startups, and buy them when the time is right. Companies like Netflix use Amazon's Amazon Web Services, and companies like Snapchat use Google's Cloud code.

This massive offering is sure to give Apple a chance at luring some of these startups to its own eco-system, and build relationships. These relationships give them the edge in partnerships and acquisitions, and also help to keep the best developers developing for iOS first.
