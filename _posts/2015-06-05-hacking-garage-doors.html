--- layout: post title: Intruders Can Open Your Garage In 9 Seconds, Using A Toy ---

Garage doors aren't secure. That's not new. What is new is that using an old toy, an intruder can open your garage door in under 10 seconds.

Why? How? I'll tell you.

If you're like many Americans, you use a fixed-code garage door. On these doors, a certain radio frequency unlocks and opens the door, allowing whoever created the frequency access to your house.

The problem is, the number of frequencies available is very limited. There are only 4096 possible codes/frequencies to unlock these garage doors. To put that in perspective, a 2-character alphanumeric password (i.e "Hi", "12") has 5184 possible combinations.

 Even worse, the way these openers work makes it easy for an intruder to try multiple codes in quick succession, making it easy for a high-tech intruder to brute-force hack their way into your garage. There's no rate-limiting. Intruders can try as many frequencies as they'd like, in as little time as they'd like.

Hacker/Blogger Samy Kamkar was able to modify a children's toy, normally used to send text messages, into a device that sends out frequencies one at a time, to open garages. The device was already fully capable of transmitting the frequencies. Kamkar simply had to program the device to send the frequencies in the right order.

Even scarier, Kamkar found a flaw that allows his device to open doors without having to guess every combination. 

The way that garage-door openers read and verify the codes they receive, if for example "030405" was sent, the opener searches for the access code by shaving off one digit at a time. For example, it would try "030", and then "304", rather than shaving off three digits at a time, and going to "405".

Using the work of Dutch mathematician De Bruijn, who discovered a sequence of numbers that cover every possible combination for a series of digits, Kamkar was able to try all 4096 combinations in 4107 bits, just 4.2% of the code required to guess combinations the traditional brute-force way. This allows his device to open any garage door of this type in under 10 seconds.

This is a remarkable security flaw, and one that many potential victims aren't aware of. You're aware now, so spread the word, and make sure you yourself are protected.
