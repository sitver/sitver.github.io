For as long as Gmail has existed, I have had multiple Gmail accounts. It helps me categorize, and compartmentalize my emails, and it allows me to sign up for multiple accounts for everything from Amazon, to Paypal.

Today I learned about two incredible, hidden Gmail features which make having multiple accounts completely unnecessary.

You're going to love these, and when you do, I hope you'll consider signing up for our Geeky digest of interesting stories, tips, and reviews.

Feature #1: Dots.

Let's say you register Johndoe@gmail.com. But then, you realize that you'd rather have a dot in your email (john.doe@gmail.com, or j.ohndoe@gmail.com).

You can add a dot anywhere in your Gmail address, or even multiple dots, and it will automatically be sent to your address.

You can, for example, use Johndoe@gmail.com and j.o.h.n.d.o.e@gmail.com interchangeably, without having to set up a new email.

This feature was designed to prevent the inevitable bounced email, when someone puts a dot where there should be none (i.e john.doe instead of johndoe), but it can also be a nifty lifehack.

You can give different emails to different people, and filter emails into separate folders based on which address they use.

You can easily separate emails from friends, co-workers, or family, keeping business and pleasure separate.

You can use this to register for multiple accounts on your favorite site. I'm sure there are many other clever uses, but I'll leave it at those, so that I can reveal to you the second, unheralded hero of a feature.

Feature #2: Plusses.

Yes. Plusses (+). Plusses work like dots, in that they allow you to make unlimited shadow emails that all forward to your Gmail account.

John, from the last example, can register for banks using johndoe+banking@gmail.com, and Geeky's digest using johndoe+geeky@gmail.com.

He can use this to create unlimited email addresses, instantly. No setup required.

You can even use this to your advantage when emailing other Gmail accounts. Want to remind someone that they owe you money? Email them with a message added to their address, like "JohnDoe+GiveMeMyMoney@gmail.com".

You can give out different addresses to different contacts. You can add in slogans (johndoe+isthebestplumber@gmail.com). You can do so many interesting things with this that I haven't even thought of.

One of my favorite uses: give different websites different email addresses, so that if one site is hacked, you don't need to throw out your email account to stop the spam. Simply block incoming emails to that particular address.

You can also use this feature for creating multiple accounts on websites like Facebook and Twitter.

These two features are really nifty, and I recommend making use of them.

(note: These features are not available on other providers, like Yahoo)
