Some people just need a physical keyboard for their smartphone. They can't stand typing on glass, or having to look while they type. 

Until now, most of those people have turned to bluetooth keyboard cases.

These keyboards work pretty well, but they're a bit heavy, you have to charge them frequently, and they're a bit of a drain on your smartphone's battery.

At New York's CE Week, I met with a company called one2TOUCH, whose solution is a bit more elegant.

{{ site.theme.header_text }}

one2TOUCH's Flipcover case runs off of NFC (Near-Field-Communication). 

NFC chips are small circuits that run off of ambient energy emitted by your smartphone. When a key is pressed, the circuit is activated, sending a signal to your phone. Bluetooth, on the other hand, is always on, always connected.

Using NFC eliminates the need for an on-keyboard battery, and the need for bluetooth on your phone. This saves weight, and energy, making one2TOUCH's covers very light.

Of course, this method has its downsides. Many phones don't have NFC technology installed. Bluetooth keyboards are cheap and readily available. The NFC keyboard is new, and not yet on the market.

Still, I think this is a promising use of NFC, and I look forward to the other ways that people will use NFC for accessories.
